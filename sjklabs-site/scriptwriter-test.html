  • Why your buyer is not seeing themselves in your story

What it is

Every memorable story has three structural ingredients: a protagonist, meaningful stakes, and dialogue that feels real. The Scriptwriter Test™ applies that same lens to authority. It is not about making a business sound more theatrical. It is about seeing whether the market can actually understand, trust, and remember what you do.

Most authority problems are story-structure problems. The expertise exists. The proof exists. But the market cannot feel the shape of it quickly enough to choose you. In SJK Labs terms, that is part of The Legibility Gap.

The three tests

When a business fails The Scriptwriter Test™, it usually fails in one of three places. Often it fails in all three.

01

The Protagonist

Are you the hero, or the guide?

Most companies position themselves as the star of the story. The buyer disappears. The market chooses the option that makes it feel most understood, not the option talking most confidently about itself.

02

The Stakes

What happens if the movie ends now?

Generic risk does not move anyone. Specific consequence does. If the cost of standing still is vague, the urgency to choose you will be vague too.

03

The Dialogue

Do you sound like a human or a manual?

Committee language is authority repellent. If your copy sounds sanitised, interchangeable or overly corporate, the market will assume the thinking behind it is interchangeable too.

Who it is for

The Scriptwriter Test™ is useful when a founder, consultant, executive team or company is clearly competent but not yet legible. You are being seen, maybe even admired, but not trusted fast enough. The market is not connecting what you do with why it matters.

"If your positioning sounds like it was written by a committee, it probably was. Real authority sounds like a person."

Typical signs
  • Your homepage explains your company, but not your buyer's win.
  • Your team knows the problem is urgent, but your market does not feel it.
  • Your copy is tidy, respectable, and easy to forget.
  • Your sales conversations go better than your positioning does.

What you walk away with

The Scriptwriter Test™ is not a decorative framework. It is a diagnostic. Used properly, it shows exactly where authority is breaking down and what needs to change before more content, outreach or visibility work will pay off.
